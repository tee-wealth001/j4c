ABOUT US

Empowering the Next Generation

Empowering the Next Generation - J4C

At J4C , we are committed to addressing and reducing the root causes of knife crime, gun crime, and gang-related violence in our communities. Our work is grounded in the belief that every young person, no matter their background or circumstances, deserves the opportunity to thrive, feel safe, and reach their full potential. We specialise in delivering solution-focused, frontline support services for marginalised and vulnerable young people, helping them navigate challenging environments and break cycles of negative behaviour. From early intervention to intensive support, we are here to make a difference where it matters most—on the ground, in homes, in schools, and in communities.

our mission

To empower vulnerable and marginalised young people by tackling the root causes of knife crime, gun crime, gang involvement, and social exclusion. We deliver practical, targeted support that strengthens families, schools, and communities, enabling young people to make positive life choices, develop healthy relationships, and break the cycle of violence and disadvantage.

our vision

A safer, more united community where all young people—regardless of background—have the opportunity to thrive. We envision a future where schools, families, and agencies work together to build resilience, reduce exclusions, and prevent youth violence through early intervention, advocacy, and community-led solutions.

Our Story

J4C was born from the urgent need to take a stand against the rising levels of youth violence and exclusion in our communities. While officially registered as a new Community Interest Company, our work is rooted in over a decade of hands-on experience through our former organisation, Safe N Sound Youth Project.

From 2014 to 2016, we delivered transformative support to NEET female ex-offenders and disadvantaged young women, helping them find pathways back into education and employment. We saw firsthand the power of combining lived experience with professional expertise—and the difference it makes when young people are supported by people who truly understand.

J4C builds on that legacy. Our team includes mentors and caseworkers with deep community ties, including ex-offenders and sector-leading professionals, who bring both credibility and compassion. We've developed a unique approach that speaks to the real challenges young people face: gang pressure, school exclusion, family breakdown, police relations, and exploitation.

Our flagship programme, New Directions New Beginnings, represents our ongoing commitment to early intervention, family engagement, and collaborative problem-solving. We believe that by working together—schools, families, community leaders, and young people—we can rewrite futures and restore hope.

Meet The Founder

Jennifer Blake

CO-FOUNDER

As a mother, grandmother, and lifelong resident of Peckham, I have dedicated my life to empowering young people and their families. My journey has been shaped by my own experiences, and I am passionate about creating positive change in our communities.

Growing up, like many young people, I faced my own struggles. Between the ages of 13 and 18, I rebelled against my parents’ efforts to guide me and was placed under the care of the local authority. I left school with no qualifications, but what I gained during that difficult period was far more important—resilience, life experience, and a deep understanding of how the decisions we make in our youth can impact our mental health and future. I’m a former student of Ivydale Primary, Gloucester Primary, and Peckham Girls School—local institutions that still hold a place in my heart. In April 2004, my life changed profoundly when I became a Christian. My faith journey led me to become an active member of Kings Church Catford, where I now serve on the pastoral support team and as a youth leader. It was through my personal journey of healing and purpose that I founded the Safe N Sound Youth Project in Peckham, with the incredible support of Harriet Harman, who helped open doors that allowed me to step into this work with greater impact. Over the years, I’ve worked with countless families, young people, and frontline professionals to address some of the most pressing issues facing our communities—youth violence, sexual exploitation, and social exclusion. I now offer my lived experience and insights to support organisations across the board, including social services, probation, schools, local and national government, and the Metropolitan Police. Out of this lifelong commitment to change and empowerment, J4C was born. Through this new chapter, I aim to continue walking alongside young people and their families—offering guidance, advocacy, and most importantly, hope. My past does not define me—it drives me. And I believe that no matter where a young person begins, with the right support, they can change their path and their future.
